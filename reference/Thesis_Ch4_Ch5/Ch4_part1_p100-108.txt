Chapter 4: Real-time Estimation of Spatially Correlated 1-D Drag Coefficient  4.1 Introduction The hexapole actuation system is a 6-input 3-output system. Since the soft magnetic material is used in the fabrication of the hexapole system, it is subject to the hysteresis effect. With the derivation and implementation of Hall-sensor-based magnetic flux control and optimal flux control, the 6-input hexapole actuation system can be treated as a linear decoupled 3-axis force producer with a bandwidth close to 4000Hz, which is experimentally verified in chapter 2. A model-based position-dependent control law was derived and implemented in chapter 3 to achieve high-speed untethered manipulation of the magnetic microprobe in aqueous solutions, where the motion error is a zero-mean random error, dictated by random thermal forces and random measurement noise. We can achieve minimum variance control simultaneously. at constant in aqueous solutions. As the probe moves close to a wall, the drag coefficient varies with its distance from the surface. The position-dependent drag coefficient is characterized by two principal directions, perpendicular and parallel to the wall [58,59]. In chapter 3, the experiment result is based on the update of drag coefficient using the theoretical value with known wall height. In this chapter, we derived a Kalman filter-based augmented estimator [56] that estimates the time-variant and/or spatially dependent input gain (drag coefficient) in the absence of the information of wall height.
