Chapter 5: Real-time Estimation of Spatially Correlated 3-D Drag Coefficient Matrix  5.1 Introduction  Real-time estimation of input gain enabled adaptive self-tuning control of the magnetic microprobe in aqueous solution. In Chapter 4, we discussed that the drag coefficient can be characterized by two principal directions, perpendicular and parallel to the surface. Thus, we can estimate the drag coefficient parallel and vertical drag coefficient respectively. This statement is true when the surface normal is overlapped with the z-direction in the measurement coordinate. In an unstructured environment, i.e., incline flat surface and sphere surface, there is no theoretical model for the wall effect. The three separate drag coefficients will turn into a  drag coefficient matrix depending on the orientation of the surface normal.  In Chapter 4, we used the variance to predict the estimated input gain for the three axes. In this Chapter, the first objective is to estimate the orientation of the surface. We utilize the covariance matrix of the tracking error to predict and estimate the orientation of the surface through real-time Principal Component Analysis [60]. Since the covariance matrix is positive definite symmetric matrix, it can be expressed in the form of diagonalized eigenvalues and eigenvectors [66]. The eigenvectors are characterized by three angles, and we derived a relationship that we can real-time update the eigenvector and the eigenvalue.
