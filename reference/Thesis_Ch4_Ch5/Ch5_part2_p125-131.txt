Instead, we estimated the orientation of the surface, utilizing the covariance matrix of the tracking error to predict and estimate the orientation of the surface through real-time principal component analysis [60]. Since the covariance matrix is positive definite

symmetric matrix, it can be written in the form of eigenvectors which are characterized by three angles and diagonalized eigenvalues.   By multiplying the eigenvector matrix, we can transform the system from the measurement coordinate to the local coordinate. The  input matrix of the scanning microprobe in the measurement coordinate is diagonalized in the local coordinate. The control law and augmented estimator can be derived similarly to the method discussed in Chapter 4. The estimation of the orientation of the surface is also included in the augmented estimator.  Two experiments were conducted to verify the control and estimation of the scanning microprobe in the designed scenario. We can correctly estimate the orientation of the tilted coverslip no matter if we rotate the coverslip in the x or y axis. The tracking error in the local coordinate is zero-mean. The estimated normalized input gain followed the real input gain tightly, and the estimated disturbance is subjected to the thermal force and measurement noise. This lays a foundation for the ability of scanning and indenting 3-D objects in the workspace.
